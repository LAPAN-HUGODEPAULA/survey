## 4. Platform Description

The Survey platform consists of applications with distinct and complementary functions.

**Survey Patient** is a module aimed at initial screening, with a brief questionnaire, generation of a preliminary summary, and the possibility of referral for professional evaluation. This module was designed to reduce barriers to access and may operate, in its initial stage, without mandatory user identification.

**Survey Frontend** is a professional-use module intended for authorized users, providing access to structured questionnaires, record registration, history tracking, and issuance of reports or documents compatible with the platform’s workflow. Access may depend on authentication.

**Clinical Narrative** is a clinical documentation module with a conversational interface, voice capture features, transcription, writing assistance, document generation, and export functions.

The platform may also include administrative, management, and configuration components, including for creating, editing, maintaining, and organizing forms, questionnaires, templates, workflows, and data.

## 5. Platform Purposes

## 8. Essential Definitions

For the purposes of this document, the following definitions apply, among others:

**Personal data:** information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
**Sensitive personal data:** data concerning racial or ethnic origin, religious belief, political opinion, trade union membership, health or sex life, genetic or biometric data, when linked to a natural person.
**Data subject:** the natural person to whom the personal data being processed relate.
**Controller:** the natural or legal person responsible for decisions regarding the processing of personal data.
**Processor:** the natural or legal person who processes personal data on behalf of the controller.
**Data Protection Officer:** the person appointed to act as a communication channel between the controller, the data subjects, and the competent authority.
**Processing:** any operation performed on personal data, such as collection, receipt, classification, use, access, reproduction, transmission, processing, filing, storage, deletion, evaluation, control, modification, communication, transfer, or extraction.
**Pseudonymization:** processing through which data lose their direct and immediate link to the data subject without the use of additional information kept separately and under control.
**Anonymization:** the use of reasonable means to prevent the direct or indirect association of data with a person.
